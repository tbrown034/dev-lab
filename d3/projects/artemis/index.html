Launch

The Space Launch System rocket lifts off from Kennedy Space Center's Launch Complex 39B, carrying four astronauts inside the Orion spacecraft.

Earth Orbit

Orion enters an initial orbit around Earth, where the crew checks all spacecraft systems before committing to the journey to the Moon.

Translunar Injection

The upper stage fires one final time, accelerating Orion to 24,500 mph and sending it on a trajectory toward the Moon — the fastest any humans have traveled since Apollo.

Lunar Flyby

Orion passes just 80 miles above the lunar surface — close enough to see boulders in craters. The Moon's gravity bends the flight path and slings the spacecraft back toward Earth.

Return & Splashdown

After 10 days in space, Orion re-enters Earth's atmosphere at 25,000 mph. Its heat shield endures 5,000°F before parachutes deploy for a Pacific Ocean splashdown.

Artemis II is the first crewed lunar mission in over 50 years — and the first step toward landing astronauts on the Moon with Artemis III.
